Culture & diversity

Ethnic grocery access and overall community diversity, by location. Ratings are relative to each other in this list, not a national scale.

The standout: Nolensville Pike, Nashville. Antioch sits directly on this corridor — home to the largest Kurdish population in the U.S., plus dense Middle Eastern, Southeast Asian, West African, and Latin American markets and restaurants packed into a few miles. It's a real culinary draw, not just a footnote, and it's the single most diverse stretch of anything in this comparison, NC included.
Charlotte & Raleigh both have strong built-in diversity. Charlotte is NC's most ethnically diverse city, with 17% of residents foreign-born. Raleigh/the Triangle has a fast-growing Asian population with its own international grocery scene (H Mart, A&C, Fresh International Market). Both are diverse in-city, no special trip required.
The Williamson County cluster is the weak point. Franklin, Brentwood, Thompson's Station, and Spring Hill all have mainstream grocery only — Kroger, Whole Foods, Trader Joe's. Real ethnic shopping means a 25-35 minute drive to the Nolensville Pike corridor. This tracks with why those towns are also the safest and most homogeneous.
